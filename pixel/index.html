Clean up an upscaled pixel-art screenshot by hand. Zoom in, cut the extra rows and columns until every block lines up with the bold grid, then reduce by that step to one pixel per block. Everything runs in your browser.

Drop an image here, click to browse, or paste from the clipboard.

Cut removes the highlighted strip and joins the two sides; add duplicates it and shifts everything else over one. To pan, use the pan tool, drag with the middle or right mouse button, or hold H and drag. Scroll to zoom. Ctrl/Cmd+Z undoes.

palette (used for snapping and source cleanup)

One hex color per line. snaps every pixel in the image to its nearest listed color, which sharpens the blurry block edges from compression. This is undoable.

Workflow. Set the bold step to the original upscale factor (4 in the wplace example). Line the bold grid up with a block edge using the offsets, then cut any block that runs a pixel wide or tall until every block fills exactly one bold cell. The alignment readout turns green when the width and height are clean multiples of the step. Then reduce.
